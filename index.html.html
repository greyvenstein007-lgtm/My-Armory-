Ballistics

Reference calculator · confirm at range
ℹ️ How to use Ballistics
▼
1
Range Card — Enter your load's muzzle velocity (MV), G1 ballistic coefficient (BC), zero distance, and sight height. Add wind speed and clock direction (12 = head-on, 3/9 = full value, 6 = tail). Tap Calculate to generate a full drop and drift table.
2
Units toggle — Switch between yd / inches and m / mm using the toggle top-right. The zero and sight height values convert automatically.
3
Reading the table — Path shows bullet height above/below line of sight (green = above, red = below). Vel is remaining velocity. ToF is time of flight in seconds. Wind is drift at that range.
4
ES / SD Calculator — Paste your chronograph readings, one per line. Tap Calculate to get Average velocity, Extreme Spread (ES), and Standard Deviation (SD). SD under 10fps = excellent. 10–15fps = good. Over 20fps = needs work.
5
Save your card — After calculating, tap 💾 Save Card to store it for offline reference at the range.
💡 Tip: Always verify your calculated holds with live fire at the range. Start at 100m/yd, confirm your zero, then step out to longer distances. Use the DOPE Book to record your confirmed real-world holds — they are more accurate than any calculator.
⚠️ Reference only. Atmospheric conditions, barrel wear, bullet lot variation and many other factors affect real-world performance. Never rely solely on calculated data for critical shots.

DOPE Book

Manual field data — confirm at range
ℹ️ How to use the DOPE Book
▼
1
What is DOPE? — Data On Previous Engagements. Your personal record of real confirmed holds — more accurate than any calculator because it reflects your exact rifle, barrel, and ammo lot.
2
Adding entries — Shoot at a known distance, dial in until on target, then tap + Entry and record the elevation and wind holds that worked. Repeat for every distance.
3
Editing entries — Every entry has an ✏️ Edit button. Tap to re-open with all fields pre-filled, change any value, then tap Update Entry to save. Tap ✕ to delete.
4
MOA vs MIL — Use whichever your scope uses.
1 MOA = 2.9cm @ 100m · 1.047" @ 100yd
1 MIL = 10cm @ 100m · 3.6" @ 100yd
1 MIL = 3.438 MOA · 1 MOA = 0.291 MIL
5
Wind holds — Record for a full-value 10mph wind (3 or 9 o'clock). Scale: 3/9 = 100%, diagonal = 50%, 12 or 6 = 0%. Multiply by actual speed ÷ 10.
6
Velocity Calc — Paste chrono readings one per line for AVG, ES and SD. SD under 10fps = excellent. Over 20fps = load needs attention.
💡 SA Tip: Temperatures swing 15°C+ between SA winter mornings and summer afternoons — velocity and drop shift noticeably. Record DOPE in both conditions for critical distances.
